# GDPR Core Principles

## Topic

General Data Protection Regulation, GDPR, core principles and responsibilities.

This source is used for questions about GDPR, personal data protection, GDPR principles, legal basis, accountability, data minimisation, transparency, integrity and confidentiality, and the connection between GDPR and cybersecurity.

## Main authority

In Sweden, GDPR and personal data protection are supervised by IMY, Integritetsskyddsmyndigheten.

GDPR itself is an EU regulation. The official legal text is Regulation (EU) 2016/679.

CyberLex Sweden should use IMY as the main Swedish authority source for GDPR supervision and the EU GDPR Regulation as the main legal source for GDPR principles.

## Key idea

GDPR regulates how personal data may be processed.

The regulation protects natural persons when their personal data is handled by organizations, companies, authorities, and other controllers.

GDPR is based on core principles that guide how personal data should be collected, used, stored, protected, and documented.

CyberLex Sweden should explain that GDPR is not only about data breaches. It also sets general rules for lawful, fair, transparent, limited, accurate, secure, and accountable processing of personal data.

## Important points

GDPR includes principles such as:

- lawfulness, fairness, and transparency
- purpose limitation
- data minimisation
- accuracy
- storage limitation
- integrity and confidentiality
- accountability

Organizations that process personal data must have a legal basis and must protect the personal data they handle.

A controller must also be able to show that it follows the GDPR principles. This is connected to accountability.

## GDPR principles explained

Lawfulness, fairness, and transparency means that personal data must be processed in a lawful, fair, and clear way.

Purpose limitation means that personal data should be collected for specific, explicit, and legitimate purposes.

Data minimisation means that organizations should only process personal data that is necessary for the purpose.

Accuracy means that personal data should be correct and kept up to date where necessary.

Storage limitation means that personal data should not be kept longer than necessary.

Integrity and confidentiality means that personal data must be protected with appropriate security.

Accountability means that the controller must be able to demonstrate compliance with GDPR.

## Cybersecurity connection

GDPR is relevant to cybersecurity because poor security can lead to personal data breaches.

Organizations need appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data.

Security measures can include access control, logging, backups, encryption, incident response routines, staff training, vulnerability management, and secure system configuration.

The GDPR principle of integrity and confidentiality is especially connected to cybersecurity because it requires personal data to be protected against unauthorized or unlawful processing, accidental loss, destruction, or damage.

## Swedish summary

GDPR är EU:s dataskyddsförordning.

GDPR reglerar hur personuppgifter får behandlas av organisationer, företag, myndigheter och andra personuppgiftsansvariga.

I Sverige är IMY, Integritetsskyddsmyndigheten, tillsynsmyndighet för GDPR och personuppgiftsskydd.

GDPR bygger på flera grundläggande principer:

- laglighet, korrekthet och öppenhet
- ändamålsbegränsning
- uppgiftsminimering
- korrekthet
- lagringsminimering
- integritet och konfidentialitet
- ansvarsskyldighet

Laglighet, korrekthet och öppenhet betyder att personuppgifter ska behandlas på ett lagligt, rättvist och tydligt sätt.

Ändamålsbegränsning betyder att personuppgifter ska samlas in för tydliga och berättigade ändamål.

Uppgiftsminimering betyder att organisationen bara ska behandla de personuppgifter som behövs för ändamålet.

Korrekthet betyder att personuppgifter ska vara riktiga och vid behov uppdaterade.

Lagringsminimering betyder att personuppgifter inte ska sparas längre än nödvändigt.

Integritet och konfidentialitet betyder att personuppgifter ska skyddas med lämpliga säkerhetsåtgärder.

Ansvarsskyldighet betyder att den personuppgiftsansvariga ska kunna visa att GDPR följs.

GDPR är relevant för cybersäkerhet eftersom bristande säkerhet kan leda till personuppgiftsincidenter.
