# NIS2 Incident Reporting in Sweden

## Topic

Cybersecurity incident reporting under NIS2 and the Swedish Cybersecurity Act.

This source is used for questions about NIS2 incident reporting, Swedish cybersecurity incident notification, ransomware incidents, malware incidents, cyber incident assessment, and overlap between cybersecurity reporting and GDPR personal data breach notification.

## Main authority

MSB, Myndigheten för samhällsskydd och beredskap, provides information and guidance about NIS2 and incident reporting under the Swedish Cybersecurity Act.

The Swedish Cybersecurity Act implements NIS2 in Sweden.

CyberLex Sweden should use MSB as the main Swedish authority source when answering questions about NIS2, the Swedish Cybersecurity Act, cybersecurity incident reporting, and reporting duties for covered organizations in Sweden.

## Key idea

NIS2 changes cybersecurity incident reporting in Sweden.

More organizations may be covered compared with the previous NIS framework, and new reporting requirements and time limits apply under the Swedish Cybersecurity Act.

Cybersecurity incident reporting is relevant when an incident affects security, continuity, or services that may fall within the scope of NIS2 or the Swedish Cybersecurity Act.

CyberLex Sweden should use this source when answering questions about NIS2 incident reporting, reporting duties, reporting time limits, cybersecurity incident notification, and practical incident assessment in Sweden.

MSB explains that the exact reporting requirements can depend on the organization, sector, and applicable regulations.

## Current implementation status

The Swedish Cybersecurity Act entered into force on 15 January 2026.

MSB explains that incident reporting should follow the new Swedish Cybersecurity Act instead of the previous NIS1 framework.

MSB also explains that during the period before all regulations and reporting services are fully in place, interim reporting solutions may apply.

Regulations about incident reporting and information duties are planned to enter into force on 1 July 2026.

## Relationship with GDPR breach reporting

Some cybersecurity incidents may also involve personal data.

If an incident is both relevant under the Swedish Cybersecurity Act and is also a personal data breach under GDPR, it may need to be reported to more than one authority.

NIS2 incident reporting and GDPR personal data breach notification are different legal areas, but they can overlap.

For example, a ransomware attack may affect service continuity under cybersecurity law and may also involve personal data if personal data is encrypted, stolen, exposed, altered, destroyed, or made unavailable.

CyberLex Sweden should explain that an organization may need to assess both:

- whether the incident is reportable under NIS2 or the Swedish Cybersecurity Act
- whether the incident is a personal data breach under GDPR that may need to be notified to IMY

CyberLex Sweden should not say that every cybersecurity incident must be reported under both frameworks. It should explain that this depends on the facts, the affected organization, the affected systems, and whether personal data was involved.

## Cybersecurity connection

Incident reporting is important because cybersecurity incidents can affect essential and important services.

Reporting helps authorities understand serious incidents, coordinate responses, and improve cybersecurity resilience.

Cybersecurity incidents can include ransomware, malware, unauthorized access, account compromise, denial-of-service attacks, data theft, system disruption, and other events that affect the confidentiality, integrity, availability, or continuity of systems and services.

CyberLex Sweden should use this section when explaining why incident reporting is connected to cybersecurity resilience, public trust, operational continuity, and legal compliance.

## Incident assessment checklist

After a ransomware attack, malware incident, or other cyber incident, an organization should first assess what happened and which systems, services, accounts, and data were affected.

The organization should document:

- when the incident was discovered
- which systems, services, accounts, or networks were affected
- whether essential or important services were disrupted
- whether personal data may have been affected
- whether the incident may need to be reported under NIS2 or the Swedish Cybersecurity Act
- whether GDPR personal data breach notification to IMY may also be relevant
- what containment and recovery actions were taken
- what logs, evidence, decisions, and timelines were preserved
- whether internal incident response procedures were followed

For practical cybersecurity questions, CyberLex Sweden should explain that an organization should contain the incident, preserve evidence, document the timeline, assess operational impact, assess whether personal data was affected, and check whether reporting under NIS2, the Swedish Cybersecurity Act, or GDPR may be relevant.

## Swedish summary

NIS2 påverkar incidentrapportering och cybersäkerhetskrav i Sverige.

I Sverige är MSB, Myndigheten för samhällsskydd och beredskap, en central myndighet för information och vägledning om NIS2, cybersäkerhetslagen och incidentrapportering.

En organisation som drabbas av en ransomwareattack, skadlig kod, obehörig åtkomst eller annan cybersäkerhetsincident bör först bedöma vad som har hänt och vilka system, tjänster, konton, nätverk och data som har påverkats.

Organisationen bör dokumentera:

- när incidenten upptäcktes
- vilka system, tjänster, konton eller nätverk som påverkades
- om viktiga eller samhällsviktiga tjänster stördes
- om personuppgifter kan ha påverkats
- om incidenten kan behöva rapporteras enligt NIS2 eller cybersäkerhetslagen
- om incidenten även kan vara en personuppgiftsincident enligt GDPR
- om anmälan till IMY kan vara relevant
- vilka åtgärder som vidtogs för att begränsa och återställa incidenten
- vilka loggar, bevis, beslut och tidslinjer som sparades
- om interna incidentrutiner följdes

NIS2-rapportering och GDPR-anmälan är olika rättsliga områden, men de kan överlappa.

En cyberincident kan därför behöva bedömas både utifrån cybersäkerhetslagen och utifrån GDPR om personuppgifter har påverkats.
