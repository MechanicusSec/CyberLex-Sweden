# Swedish Cybercrime: Dataintrång

## Topic

Dataintrång, data intrusion, under Swedish criminal law.

This source is used for questions about dataintrång, unauthorized access, unlawful access to information systems, Swedish cybercrime law, account compromise, hacking, interference with data or systems, and why penetration testing requires clear permission.

## Main legal source

Brottsbalken, the Swedish Penal Code, is the main legal source for dataintrång.

CyberLex Sweden should use Riksdagen and official Swedish legal material when answering questions about dataintrång and Swedish cybercrime law.

CyberLex Sweden should explain that dataintrång is a Swedish criminal-law concept and that the exact legal assessment depends on the facts, the conduct, authorization, intent, and the applicable legal provision.

## Key idea

Dataintrång is one of the central Swedish criminal law provisions related to attacks against information systems.

It is connected to unauthorized access to, or interference with, data or information systems.

In cybersecurity terms, dataintrång is relevant when discussing hacking, unauthorized access, account compromise, unlawful use of credentials, and unlawful interference with computer systems or data.

CyberLex Sweden should explain dataintrång at a high level and should not provide instructions for committing cybercrime.

## Legal reference

Dataintrång is regulated in Chapter 4 Section 9 c of Brottsbalken.

The penalty scale for dataintrång can include fines or imprisonment.

More serious cases may be treated as gross data intrusion, grovt dataintrång.

CyberLex Sweden should explain that the legal classification can depend on the seriousness of the conduct, the affected systems, the impact, and other facts.

CyberLex Sweden should not give a final legal conclusion about whether a specific act is criminal. It should explain the general rule and refer users to official legal sources or qualified legal advice for real cases.

## Practical explanation

In cybersecurity terms, dataintrång can be relevant when someone accesses, changes, deletes, blocks, copies, or interferes with data or systems without permission.

This can include situations such as:

- unauthorized account access
- hacking into a system
- malicious interference with data
- unauthorized use of credentials
- unlawful access to protected systems
- copying or extracting data without authorization
- blocking or disrupting access to data or systems
- using another person's account without permission

CyberLex Sweden should explain that authorization is central. Security testing, access, scanning, exploitation, or use of credentials can create legal risk if it is done without permission or outside the agreed scope.

CyberLex Sweden should keep the explanation educational and defensive.

## Penetration testing connection

Penetration testing and security testing should only be performed with clear permission.

Without authorization, activities such as accessing systems, scanning aggressively, exploiting vulnerabilities, bypassing access controls, copying data, or using credentials may create legal risk.

Ethical hacking requires permission, a defined scope, agreed methods, and clear documentation.

A penetration test should normally define:

- who has authorized the test
- which systems are in scope
- which systems are out of scope
- what testing methods are allowed
- what time period the test covers
- how findings should be reported
- how sensitive data should be handled
- who should be contacted if something goes wrong

CyberLex Sweden should explain that penetration testing without clear permission can move from security work into legal risk territory. Because naturally, humans invented both hacking and paperwork, then made one illegal without the other.

## Cybersecurity connection

Dataintrång is relevant to cybersecurity because unauthorized access and interference with systems are common parts of cyber incidents.

Examples can include compromised accounts, stolen credentials, exploitation of vulnerabilities, unauthorized access to databases, manipulation of data, or disruption of systems.

Dataintrång can also overlap with other cybersecurity and compliance issues.

For example:

- unauthorized access may also cause a personal data breach under GDPR if personal data is affected
- an incident may require technical investigation and evidence preservation
- an organization may need to assess whether other reporting duties are relevant
- logs, timelines, access records, and affected systems may be important for investigation

## Swedish summary

Dataintrång är ett svenskt straffrättsligt begrepp som rör obehörig åtkomst till eller obehörig påverkan på data eller informationssystem.

Dataintrång regleras i brottsbalken.

I cybersäkerhetssammanhang kan dataintrång vara relevant vid frågor om:

- obehörig åtkomst
- hackning
- kontokapning
- användning av inloggningsuppgifter utan tillstånd
- intrång i system
- ändring, radering eller blockering av data
- kopiering eller åtkomst till data utan behörighet
- påverkan på system eller information

Behörighet och tillstånd är mycket viktigt.

Om någon testar, skannar, angriper, loggar in i eller använder ett system utan tydligt tillstånd kan det innebära juridisk risk.

Penetrationstestning och säkerhetstestning bör därför bara göras med tydligt godkännande och tydlig omfattning.

Ett tillåtet säkerhetstest bör normalt ha:

- tydlig beställare eller uppdragsgivare
- tydlig behörighet
- tydlig omfattning
- tydliga system som ingår i testet
- tydliga system som inte ingår i testet
- tydliga regler för metoder
- tydliga regler för hantering av känslig information
- tydlig rapportering av resultat
