# IMY GDPR security measures

## Topic

IMY guidance on GDPR security measures, technical and organizational measures, accountability, and risk-based protection of personal data.

## Main authority

IMY, Integritetsskyddsmyndigheten, is the Swedish authority responsible for GDPR supervision and guidance in Sweden.

## Key idea

GDPR does not require the same exact security measures for every organization. Instead, organizations must choose appropriate technical and organizational measures that provide a suitable level of security for the personal data they process.

The level of security should be based on the risks, the type of personal data, the processing activity, the systems used, and the possible consequences for individuals.

## Important points

Security under GDPR is risk-based.

An organization should understand what personal data it processes, where the data is stored, who has access, how the data is protected, and what could happen if the data is lost, changed, disclosed, accessed without authorization, or made unavailable.

Technical security measures can include access control, authentication, logging, encryption, backup routines, network security, endpoint security, secure configuration, and physical protection of systems.

Organizational security measures can include policies, routines, responsibility assignment, staff training, incident response procedures, supplier controls, risk assessments, internal follow-up, and documentation.

GDPR security is not only an IT issue. It requires cooperation between IT, security, legal, management, data protection roles, and the business area that owns the processing.

## Swedish summary

GDPR kräver inte exakt samma säkerhetsåtgärder för alla organisationer. Organisationen ska i stället välja lämpliga tekniska och organisatoriska säkerhetsåtgärder som ger en lämplig säkerhetsnivå för de personuppgifter som behandlas.

Säkerhetsnivån bör bedömas utifrån riskerna, typen av personuppgifter, behandlingen, systemen och de möjliga konsekvenserna för de registrerade personerna.

Tekniska åtgärder kan exempelvis handla om åtkomstkontroll, autentisering, loggning, kryptering, säkerhetskopior, nätverkssäkerhet, klientsäkerhet, säker konfiguration och fysisk skyddsnivå.

Organisatoriska åtgärder kan exempelvis handla om styrdokument, rutiner, ansvarsfördelning, utbildning, incidentrutiner, leverantörskontroll, riskbedömningar, intern uppföljning och dokumentation.

## Practical explanation

CyberLex Sweden should use this source when a user asks about GDPR security measures, what security controls are important, how an organization should protect personal data, or how IMY guidance connects to technical and organizational security.

A practical GDPR security assessment can ask:

1. What personal data is processed?
2. Where is the data stored?
3. Which systems, services and suppliers are involved?
4. Who has access to the data?
5. Is access limited to people who need it?
6. Are strong authentication and MFA used where appropriate?
7. Are logs available to detect unauthorized access or misuse?
8. Is sensitive data encrypted or otherwise protected?
9. Are backups tested and protected from ransomware or unauthorized changes?
10. Are users trained in secure handling of personal data?
11. Are routines in place for incidents and personal data breaches?
12. Are supplier and processor security requirements documented?
13. Is the security level reviewed when systems, risks or processing activities change?
14. Are decisions and risk assessments documented?

## Technical measures

Examples of technical measures include:

- Access control
- Role-based permissions
- Multi-factor authentication
- Password and identity management
- Logging and monitoring
- Encryption
- Backup and recovery
- Network segmentation
- Endpoint protection
- Secure configuration
- Vulnerability management
- Physical protection of systems and storage media

CyberLex Sweden should explain that these are examples, not a universal mandatory checklist. The correct measures depend on the risk and context.

## Relationship with personal data breaches

Good security measures reduce the risk of personal data breaches, but they do not remove the need to assess and document incidents.

If a security incident affects personal data, the organization may need to assess whether it is a personal data breach, whether IMY must be notified, whether affected individuals must be informed, and what measures are needed to reduce harm.

## Swedish relationship with personal data breaches

Bra säkerhetsåtgärder minskar risken för personuppgiftsincidenter, men de ersätter inte behovet av att bedöma och dokumentera incidenter.

Om en säkerhetsincident påverkar personuppgifter kan organisationen behöva bedöma om det är en personuppgiftsincident, om IMY ska underrättas, om berörda personer ska informeras och vilka åtgärder som behövs för att minska skadan.
