# EU Cyber Resilience Act

## Topic

Cybersecurity requirements for products with digital elements.

## Main authority

The official EU legal source is Regulation (EU) 2024/2847, known as the Cyber Resilience Act.

## Key idea

The Cyber Resilience Act introduces cybersecurity requirements for products with digital elements.

It is relevant to manufacturers, importers, distributors, and other actors involved with products that include digital components.

## Important points

The Cyber Resilience Act focuses on cybersecurity requirements across the lifecycle of digital products.

It is relevant to topics such as:

- secure product design
- vulnerability handling
- cybersecurity requirements
- products with digital elements
- manufacturer responsibilities
- EU market requirements

## Swedish connection

The Cyber Resilience Act is EU law and is relevant to Sweden because EU regulations apply across Member States.

CyberLex Sweden should use this source when answering questions about cybersecurity obligations for products with digital elements.

## Cybersecurity connection

The regulation connects cybersecurity law with product security.

It helps explain that cybersecurity responsibility is not only about incident response, but also about designing and maintaining secure digital products.
