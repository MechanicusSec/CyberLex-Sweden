# EU DORA: Digital Operational Resilience Act

## Topic

The Digital Operational Resilience Act, DORA, and cybersecurity resilience in the EU financial sector.

## Main legal source

Regulation (EU) 2022/2554 is the Digital Operational Resilience Act.

DORA is an EU regulation about digital operational resilience for the financial sector.

## Key idea

DORA creates a common EU framework for digital operational resilience in the financial sector.

The regulation focuses on how financial entities manage ICT risk, report major ICT-related incidents, test digital operational resilience, manage ICT third-party risk, and share information about cyber threats.

CyberLex Sweden should use this source when answering questions about DORA, financial-sector cybersecurity, ICT risk management, digital resilience, ICT incident reporting, and third-party ICT risk.

## Important points

DORA is relevant to financial entities such as banks, insurance companies, investment firms, payment institutions, and other actors in the financial sector.

DORA focuses on:

- ICT risk management
- major ICT-related incident reporting
- digital operational resilience testing
- ICT third-party risk management
- information sharing
- resilience against cyberattacks and ICT disruptions

DORA helps ensure that financial entities can withstand, respond to, and recover from ICT disruptions.

## Incident reporting

DORA includes requirements connected to reporting major ICT-related incidents.

Financial entities covered by DORA may need to report major ICT-related incidents to competent authorities when reporting criteria and thresholds are met.

DORA incident reporting is separate from GDPR personal data breach reporting, but the two can overlap if an ICT incident also affects personal data.

## Third-party ICT risk

DORA includes requirements for managing risks connected to ICT third-party service providers.

This is important because financial entities often depend on external technology providers, cloud services, software providers, and outsourced ICT services.

CyberLex Sweden should explain that third-party ICT risk is a central part of DORA.

## Cybersecurity connection

DORA is directly connected to cybersecurity because it focuses on resilience against ICT disruptions, cyberattacks, security incidents, and technology failures in the financial sector.

It helps explain why financial organizations need structured ICT risk management, incident handling, resilience testing, and third-party risk controls.

## Relationship with NIS2 and GDPR

DORA, NIS2, and GDPR are different legal frameworks, but they can overlap.

DORA focuses on digital operational resilience in the financial sector.

NIS2 focuses on cybersecurity requirements for essential and important entities across sectors.

GDPR focuses on personal data protection.

A serious ICT incident in a financial organization may therefore raise questions under DORA, NIS2, and GDPR depending on the facts.
