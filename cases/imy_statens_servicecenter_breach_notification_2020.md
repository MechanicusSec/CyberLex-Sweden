# Statens Servicecenter Breach Notification Case

## Case type

IMY decision / GDPR personal data breach / processor duties / delayed notification

## Jurisdiction

Sweden

## Year

2020

## Authority or court

IMY, Integritetsskyddsmyndigheten

## Topic

GDPR, personal data breach, processor duties, controller notification, delayed breach notification, public sector, administrative fine.

## Short summary

IMY issued an administrative fine against Statens servicecenter after reviewing how the authority handled a personal data breach.

The case concerned Statens servicecenter in its role as a personal data processor. IMY found that Statens servicecenter had delayed notifying the relevant data controllers about discovered security deficiencies.

Statens servicecenter was also late in notifying IMY about its own personal data breach.

This case is useful for CyberLex Sweden because it shows that breach handling duties can apply not only to controllers, but also to processors that handle personal data on behalf of others.

Statens servicecenter var också sen med att anmäla den egna personuppgiftsincidenten till IMY.

## What happened

Statens servicecenter processed personal data on behalf of other public authorities.

IMY reviewed how Statens servicecenter handled discovered security deficiencies and a personal data breach.

The authority found that Statens servicecenter, as a personal data processor, had delayed notifying the relevant controllers about discovered security deficiencies.

IMY also found that Statens servicecenter was late in notifying IMY about its own personal data breach.

## Swedish what happened

## Legal issue

The main legal issue was whether Statens servicecenter had fulfilled its GDPR duties when handling a personal data breach and when notifying controllers about discovered security deficiencies.

The case is relevant to breach notification, processor obligations, controller-processor relationships, incident escalation, documentation, and the timing of notification.

## Decision or outcome

IMY issued an administrative fine against Statens servicecenter.

The case shows that incident handling delays can create GDPR risk. A processor should notify the controller without undue delay when it becomes aware of a personal data breach or relevant security deficiencies.

The case also shows that an organization must handle its own notification duties carefully when it is itself responsible for notifying IMY.

## Swedish decision or outcome

IMY beslutade om en administrativ sanktionsavgift mot Statens servicecenter.

Fallet visar att förseningar i incidenthantering kan skapa GDPR-risk. Ett personuppgiftsbiträde bör underrätta den personuppgiftsansvariga utan onödigt dröjsmål när biträdet får kännedom om en personuppgiftsincident eller relevanta säkerhetsbrister.

Fallet visar också att en organisation måste hantera sina egna anmälningsskyldigheter noggrant när den själv ansvarar för att anmäla till IMY.

Official fine:

SEK 200,000

CyberLex should not present this as a fine prediction for other cases.

The fine amount is case-specific and depends on the authority's assessment of the facts, the delay, the role of the organization, the type of personal data processing, the incident handling, and other relevant factors.

## Why it matters for CyberLex

This case is important for CyberLex Sweden because it shows that breach handling is not only about technical containment.

A GDPR incident workflow may also require:

* fast internal escalation
* notification from processor to controller
* notification from controller to IMY when required
* documentation of timing and decisions
* clear incident roles and responsibilities
* breach handling procedures in processor agreements
