# Miljödata Data Leak Case

## Case type

IMY review / ongoing supervision / cyberattack / personal data breach / supplier incident

## Jurisdiction

Sweden

## Year

2025

## Authority or court

IMY, Integritetsskyddsmyndigheten

## Topic

GDPR, personal data breach, cyberattack, supplier incident, processor duties, controller duties, employee data, darknet publication, incident notification, security measures.

## Short summary

In 2025, Miljödata was affected by an IT attack that led to a reported personal data leak connected to the company's services.

Miljödata provides systems and services used by many organizations, including municipalities, regions, and other public-sector actors.

The incident became especially serious because one supplier incident could affect many different controllers at the same time.

Personal data connected to the attack was later reported as published on Darknet, which increased the risk for affected individuals.

The leaked information was reported to include data about employees and former employees, and in some cases there were concerns about people with protected identities.

The case is therefore relevant not only as a technical security incident, but also as a GDPR case about supplier risk, processor responsibility, controller responsibility, and incident coordination.

IMY explained that affected organizations may need to report personal data breaches within 72 hours from when they become aware of the incident.

IMY also clarified that organizations do not need to know every detail before making an initial notification, because supplementary information can be provided later.

The case raises important questions about how controllers and processors should communicate, document decisions, assess risk, and inform affected individuals during a developing breach.

IMY later opened reviews connected to the Miljödata leak, including Miljödata and selected organizations that used the company's services.

The case is useful for CyberLex Sweden because it shows how one supplier attack can create GDPR obligations for several parties at the same time.

This is an ongoing review case, not a final administrative fine case, so CyberLex should not present it as a completed enforcement decision.

## What happened

In 2025, Miljödata was affected by an IT attack. Personal data connected to the incident was later reported as leaked.

Miljödata is a system supplier whose services were used by several organizations. This made the incident more complex than a normal single-organization breach, because the attack could affect both the supplier and the organizations using the supplier's systems.

IMY published information for affected organizations and explained that personal data breaches may need to be reported to IMY within 72 hours from when the organization becomes aware of the breach.

IMY also explained that if all information is not available at the time of notification, organizations can submit supplementary information later.

Personal data connected to the incident was later reported as published on Darknet. IMY warned affected organizations not to search for or download the leaked files, because doing so could create new personal data processing, contribute to further spread, and expose systems to possible malware.

IMY later announced that it had opened reviews based on the Miljödata leak. The reviews concern Miljödata, Göteborgs stad, Älmhults kommun, and Region Västmanland.

The review of Miljödata mainly concerns security issues connected to the intrusion. The reviews of the public-sector customers focus on their processing of personal data in Miljödata's systems, including what types of data were processed and which groups of people were affected.

IMY has also published guidance explaining that the fact that an incident occurs does not automatically mean that a controller or processor has failed in its responsibility. The assessment depends on whether the organization had an appropriate level of security in relation to the risk.

## Decision or outcome

IMY opened reviews based on the Miljödata leak.

As of this case summary, CyberLex should treat this as an ongoing review case. It should not present a final administrative fine, final fault finding, or completed enforcement outcome unless official sources later confirm such an outcome.

The case shows that a supplier incident can trigger GDPR obligations for multiple parties, including the supplier and organizations using the supplier's services.

The case also shows that IMY may examine both the supplier's security measures and the customers' own processing of personal data in the supplier's systems.

## Learning note

This case shows that incident response must handle uncertainty. Organizations may need to notify IMY before all facts are known and then provide supplementary information later.

Supplier incidents also require clear controller-processor coordination. The processor may hold or operate the affected system, while the controller may still have duties toward IMY and affected individuals.

The case also shows that leaked data should not be handled carelessly. Searching for or downloading exposed Darknet material can create new risks and may itself become a new personal data processing activity.

## Swedish learning note

Fallet visar att incidenthantering måste kunna hantera osäkerhet. Organisationer kan behöva anmäla till IMY innan alla fakta är kända och sedan komplettera med mer information senare.

Leverantörsincidenter kräver också tydlig samordning mellan personuppgiftsansvariga och personuppgiftsbiträden. Personuppgiftsbiträdet kan ha eller driva det drabbade systemet, medan personuppgiftsansvariga fortfarande kan ha skyldigheter gentemot IMY och berörda personer.

Fallet visar också att läckta uppgifter inte ska hanteras vårdslöst. Att söka efter eller ladda ned exponerat material från Darknet kan skapa nya risker och kan i sig bli en ny personuppgiftsbehandling.
