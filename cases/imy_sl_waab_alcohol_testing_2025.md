# SL and WÅAB Alcohol Testing Case

## Case type

IMY decision / GDPR workplace data / alcohol testing / employee data / data minimisation

## Jurisdiction

Sweden

## Year

2025

## Authority or court

IMY, Integritetsskyddsmyndigheten

## Topic

GDPR, workplace data, employee privacy, alcohol testing, lawful basis, data minimisation, storage limitation, public transport, administrative fine.

## Short summary

IMY issued administrative fines of SEK 75,000 against Aktiebolaget Storstockholms Lokaltrafik (SL) and SEK 75,000 against Waxholms Ångfartygs AB (WÅAB).

The case concerned personal data collected and stored in connection with alcohol tests of employees working as ship captains in public transport.

IMY found that the companies had collected and stored alcohol-test information too extensively and for too long, even though safety in public transport can in some cases justify alcohol testing.

## Swedish short summary

IMY beslutade om administrativa sanktionsavgifter på 75 000 kronor mot Aktiebolaget Storstockholms Lokaltrafik (SL) och 75 000 kronor mot Waxholms Ångfartygs AB (WÅAB).

Fallet gällde personuppgifter som samlades in och lagrades i samband med alkoholtester av arbetstagare som arbetade som befälhavare för fartyg inom kollektivtrafiken.

IMY fann att bolagen hade samlat in och lagrat uppgifter från alkoholtester i för stor omfattning och under för lång tid, även om säkerhet inom kollektivtrafik i vissa fall kan motivera alkoholtester.

## What happened

IMY reviewed two complaints from employees who had undergone alcohol tests as part of their work as ship captains in public transport.

An employer may in some cases have a legitimate interest in carrying out alcohol testing to ensure safety, for example in public transport.

However, IMY found that SL and WÅAB had collected and stored information from the alcohol tests in a way that went beyond what was necessary for the purpose.

IMY also noted that the employment context matters because employees are in a dependent position in relation to their employer.

## Swedish what happened

IMY granskade två klagomål från arbetstagare som hade genomfört alkoholtester inom ramen för sina anställningar som befälhavare för fartyg i kollektivtrafiken.

En arbetsgivare kan i vissa fall ha ett berättigat intresse av att utföra alkoholtester för att säkerställa säkerhet, till exempel inom kollektivtrafiken.

IMY fann dock att SL och WÅAB hade samlat in och lagrat uppgifter från alkoholtester på ett sätt som gick längre än vad som var nödvändigt för syftet.

IMY betonade också att arbetslivssammanhanget är viktigt eftersom arbetstagare befinner sig i en beroendeställning i förhållande till arbetsgivaren.

## Legal issue

The main legal issue was whether SL and WÅAB had legal support under the GDPR for collecting and storing personal data from employee alcohol tests, and whether the processing was necessary and proportionate.

The case is relevant to lawful basis, data minimisation, storage limitation, employee privacy, workplace testing, and handling of potentially sensitive information.

## Decision or outcome

IMY issued an administrative fine of SEK 75,000 against SL.

IMY also issued an administrative fine of SEK 75,000 against WÅAB.

The case shows that even where workplace testing may have a legitimate safety purpose, the employer must still ensure that the personal data processing is necessary, proportionate, limited, and supported by the GDPR.

## Why it matters for CyberLex

This case is important for CyberLex Sweden because it shows that GDPR risk can arise in ordinary workplace routines, not only in cyberattacks or technical incidents.

A GDPR issue can arise when an employer:

* collects more employee data than necessary
* stores employee data for too long
* fails to limit access or retention
* relies too broadly on safety justifications
* does not consider the employee's dependent position
* handles alcohol-test information without clear routines

## Learning note

This case shows that a lawful safety purpose does not automatically justify broad or long-term storage of employee personal data. Employers must still apply data minimisation, storage limitation, necessity, proportionality, and clear routines.

## Swedish learning note

Fallet visar att ett legitimt säkerhetssyfte inte automatiskt motiverar bred eller långvarig lagring av arbetstagares personuppgifter. Arbetsgivare måste fortfarande tillämpa uppgiftsminimering, lagringsminimering, nödvändighet, proportionalitet och tydliga rutiner.
