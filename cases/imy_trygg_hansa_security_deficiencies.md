# Trygg-Hansa Security Deficiencies

## Case type

IMY decision / authority decision

## Jurisdiction

Sweden

## Year

2023

## Authority or court

IMY, Integritetsskyddsmyndigheten

## Topic

GDPR, security deficiencies, customer data, unauthorized access risk, technical and organisational measures, administrative fine.

## Short summary

IMY issued an administrative fine of SEK 35 million against Trygg-Hansa after security deficiencies made information about approximately 650,000 customers accessible to unauthorized persons via the internet.

The case concerned a serious weakness in technical security, where customer information could be accessed without proper authorization.

The affected data was connected to an insurance company, which made the incident especially important because customers may share sensitive, private, or financially relevant information with insurers.

IMY found that Trygg-Hansa had not taken appropriate technical measures to ensure a level of security suitable for the risk.

The case was not mainly about a ransomware attack or an attacker stealing a database through malware.

Instead, the GDPR risk came from weak technical protection that made customer data available online.

This makes the case useful as an example of how insecure system design, weak access control, or exposed data paths can become a large-scale GDPR security issue.

The incident shows that personal data can be exposed even when there is no obvious dramatic cyberattack.

It also shows that organizations must test and verify that customer data is not accessible through predictable links, weak technical controls, or poorly protected systems.

The case is a strong example of how security weaknesses can lead to major administrative fines when large numbers of individuals are affected.

## What happened

Trygg-Hansa had security deficiencies that made customer information accessible to unauthorized persons via the internet.

According to IMY, the issue affected information about approximately 650,000 customers.

The exposed information was connected to Trygg-Hansa's customer data, which made the incident serious because insurance-related customer environments can involve private and potentially sensitive information.

The issue showed that technical access to personal data had not been limited well enough.

The case illustrates a common but dangerous type of security failure: data is not stolen through a complicated attack, but is instead exposed because a system allows access in a way it should not.

In this type of incident, the main problem is often weak authorization, poor system design, predictable access paths, or missing technical barriers.

IMY concluded that Trygg-Hansa had not taken appropriate technical measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk.

The case shows that organizations must not only store data in systems, but also ensure that those systems prevent unauthorized access in practice.

## Legal issue

The main legal issue was whether Trygg-Hansa had implemented appropriate technical and organisational security measures under GDPR.

The case connects to GDPR security requirements and the need to protect personal data against unauthorized access, exposure, and misuse.

It is especially relevant to Article 32 GDPR because the decision focused on whether the technical security level was appropriate in relation to the risk.

The case also raises practical questions about how organizations should test access controls, validate system design, and prevent customer data from being exposed online.

## Decision or outcome

IMY issued an administrative fine of SEK 35 million against Trygg-Hansa.

IMY stated that Trygg-Hansa had not taken appropriate technical measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk.

The decision shows that large-scale exposure of customer data can lead to serious GDPR consequences even when the incident does not look like a classic hacking scenario.

The case also shows that technical measures must work in practice, not only exist as general security intentions or policy statements.

Organizations are expected to verify that personal data cannot be accessed by unauthorized persons through system weaknesses.

## Learning note

This case shows that technical security weaknesses can create major GDPR consequences when customer data becomes accessible online.

Access control, secure design, authorization checks, testing, logging, and risk-based technical measures are not just IT details. They are part of data protection compliance.

Organizations should not assume that personal data is protected simply because it is stored inside an application.

They must verify that users can only access the data they are allowed to access, and that unauthorized persons cannot reach customer information through direct links, predictable identifiers, weak permissions, or exposed endpoints.

The painfully ordinary lesson, because apparently it must be repeated until civilization improves, is that “online” does not mean “secure.”

## Swedish learning note

Fallet visar att tekniska säkerhetsbrister kan få stora GDPR-konsekvenser när kunduppgifter blir tillgängliga online.

Åtkomstkontroll, säker systemdesign, behörighetskontroller, testning, loggning och riskbaserade tekniska åtgärder är inte bara IT-detaljer. De är en del av dataskyddsefterlevnaden.

Organisationer bör inte anta att personuppgifter är skyddade bara för att de finns inne i en applikation.

De måste kontrollera att användare bara kan nå de uppgifter de har rätt att nå, och att obehöriga inte kan komma åt kundinformation genom direktlänkar, förutsägbara identifierare, svaga behörigheter eller exponerade ändpunkter.

Den plågsamt vanliga lärdomen, eftersom den tydligen måste upprepas tills civilisationen förbättras, är att “online” inte betyder “säkert.”
