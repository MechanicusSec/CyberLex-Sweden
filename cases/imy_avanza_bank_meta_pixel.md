# Avanza Bank and Meta Pixel

## Case type

IMY decision / EDPB summary

## Jurisdiction

Sweden / EU

## Year

2024

## Authority or court

IMY, Integritetsskyddsmyndigheten

European Data Protection Board, EDPB

## Topic

GDPR, personal data transfer, Meta Pixel, customer data, technical and organisational measures, financial-sector data, administrative fine.

## Short summary

IMY issued an administrative fine of SEK 15 million against Avanza Bank AB after personal data from up to one million customers was transferred to Meta through the bank's use of Meta Pixel.

The issue was connected to the bank's website and app and involved incorrect handling or configuration of the tracking technology.

This case is useful for CyberLex Sweden because it shows how website or app tracking technology can create serious GDPR risk when personal data is transferred to third parties without proper control.

## Swedish short summary

IMY beslutade om en administrativ sanktionsavgift på 15 miljoner kronor mot Avanza Bank AB efter att personuppgifter från upp till en miljon kunder överfördes till Meta genom bankens användning av Meta Pixel.

Problemet var kopplat till bankens webbplats och app och gällde felaktig hantering eller konfiguration av spårningstekniken.

Fallet är användbart för CyberLex Sweden eftersom det visar hur spårningsteknik på webbplatser eller i appar kan skapa allvarlig GDPR-risk när personuppgifter överförs till tredje part utan tillräcklig kontroll.

## What happened

Avanza Bank used Meta Pixel on its website and in its app.

According to the EDPB summary, Avanza's notification stated that personal data of up to one million customers was wrongly transferred to Meta between 15 November 2019 and 2 June 2021.

IMY stated that the incorrect transfer concerned customer data and that the bank had later deactivated the pixel after becoming aware of what had happened.

IMY also stated that Avanza had developed internal routines after discovering the incorrect transfer.

## Swedish what happened

Avanza Bank använde Meta Pixel på sin webbplats och i sin app.

Enligt EDPB:s sammanfattning uppgav Avanza i sin anmälan att personuppgifter om upp till en miljon kunder felaktigt hade överförts till Meta mellan den 15 november 2019 och den 2 juni 2021.

IMY uppgav att den felaktiga överföringen gällde kunduppgifter och att banken senare stängde av pixeln efter att ha blivit medveten om vad som hade hänt.

IMY uppgav också att Avanza tog fram interna rutiner efter att den felaktiga överföringen upptäcktes.

## Legal issue

The main legal issue was whether Avanza had protected customer personal data properly when using Meta Pixel.

The case is relevant to GDPR security, confidentiality, technical and organisational measures, and control over third-party tracking technology.

## Decision or outcome

IMY issued an administrative fine of SEK 15 million against Avanza Bank AB.

The case shows that tracking technology can become a GDPR problem when it causes personal data to be transferred to a third party in an uncontrolled or unlawful way.

## Why it matters for CyberLex

This case is important for CyberLex Sweden because it shows that data leaks or GDPR problems do not only happen through hacking or ransomware.

A privacy or security incident can also happen through:

* misconfigured tracking tools
* analytics scripts

* website or app integrations
* external platforms receiving personal data
* weak internal review before deployment

## Learning note

This case shows that financial-sector data and tracking technology require strong control before deployment. A misconfigured or poorly reviewed tracking tool can become a data protection incident even when there is no traditional cyberattack.

## Swedish learning note

Fallet visar att finansiella kunduppgifter och spårningsteknik kräver stark kontroll innan de används. Ett felkonfigurerat eller otillräckligt granskat spårningsverktyg kan bli en dataskyddsincident även utan en traditionell cyberattack.
