# CyberLex Sweden Terms of Use

## Purpose

These Terms of Use describe how CyberLex Sweden should be used.

CyberLex Sweden is an educational prototype created as a final school project.

It provides simplified, source-based information about selected Swedish and EU cybersecurity-law, data protection, digital compliance, cybercrime, real-world case examples, and defensive incident-response topics.

CyberLex Sweden does not provide legal advice.

---

## Educational Use Only

CyberLex Sweden is intended for:

* education
* demonstration
* portfolio use
* local testing
* cybersecurity-law learning
* source-grounded prototype evaluation
* documentation practice
* defensive incident-response learning

The application is designed to help users understand selected topics by showing:

* source-based answers
* official source links
* source metadata
* source context
* source freshness labels
* source quality labels
* related case examples where relevant
* limitations
* defensive guidance where appropriate

It is not intended for production legal, regulatory, compliance, privacy, or incident-response use.

---

## No Legal Advice

CyberLex Sweden is not:

* a lawyer
* a law firm
* a court
* a public authority
* an official legal service
* a data protection officer
* a compliance officer
* a professional incident-response provider
* a forensic investigation team
* a replacement for internal legal, compliance, privacy, or security procedures

The information provided by CyberLex Sweden should not be treated as legal advice.

Users should verify important legal, compliance, regulatory, privacy, and cybersecurity questions with:

* official legal sources
* relevant public authorities
* qualified legal professionals
* data protection officers
* compliance specialists
* professional cybersecurity or incident-response teams
* current laws and regulations
* internal organizational policies and procedures

---

## Limited Scope

CyberLex Sweden only covers selected cybersecurity-related legal, compliance, case-library, and defensive incident-response topics.

Current supported areas include selected material related to:

* GDPR and personal data breaches
* GDPR security measures
* IMY and Swedish GDPR supervision
* GDPR core principles
* NIS2 and Swedish cybersecurity law
* NIS2 sector scope and applicability
* NIS2 incident reporting
* Swedish cybercrime law and dataintrång
* EU attacks against information systems
* Cyber Resilience Act
* DORA and digital operational resilience
* defensive cybersecurity incident response
* selected GDPR and cybersecurity-related case examples

Questions outside the project scope may be refused.

CyberLex Sweden does not cover all Swedish law, all EU law, all cybersecurity compliance requirements, or all possible incident-response situations.

---

## User Responsibility

Users are responsible for how they interpret and use the information provided by CyberLex Sweden.

Users should not rely on CyberLex Sweden as the only source for:

* legal decisions
* compliance decisions
* privacy decisions
* regulatory reporting
* breach notification decisions
* cybersecurity incident handling
* operational security decisions
* business risk decisions
* financial or sanction risk decisions

Users should verify important matters with official sources and qualified professionals.

CyberLex Sweden can help with learning and structured thinking. It does not transfer responsibility away from the user, organization, or relevant professionals. Tragic, but reality still has paperwork.

## Prohibited Use

CyberLex Sweden must not be used to:

* commit cybercrime
* plan unauthorized access
* bypass security controls
* exploit systems without authorization
* steal credentials
* hide logs or traces
* avoid detection
* maintain unauthorized access
* attack systems
* deploy malware
* perform phishing
* evade security tools
* avoid legal responsibility
* harass, threaten, or harm others
* replace professional legal advice
* make high-risk legal or operational decisions without verification

CyberLex Sweden may explain cybersecurity-law concepts in an educational and lawful context.

It may provide defensive incident-response guidance for supported scenarios.

The application should refuse unsafe cyber requests.

---

## Source-Based Answers

CyberLex Sweden attempts to answer only from local trusted knowledge base files.

The main knowledge base is stored in:

```text
data/
```

The application may display:

* citation details
* matched source file
* matched source section
* official source links
* source metadata
* source freshness labels
* source quality labels
* matched source excerpts
* relevant source context

If no trusted source is found, CyberLex Sweden should refuse to answer confidently.

Source-grounding improves transparency, but it does not guarantee legal completeness or current legal validity.

The guiding rule is:

```text
No trusted source, no answer.
```

---

## Case Library Use

CyberLex Sweden may show related case examples from local case-library files stored in:

```text
cases/
```

The case library may include:

* public authority decisions
* public incident examples
* GDPR-related cases
* cybersecurity-related cases
* fines or outcomes where known
* learning notes
* related CyberLex questions
* official or reliable source links

Case examples are educational context only.

They are not:

* legal predictions
* fine predictions
* complete legal analysis
* proof that another case would have the same result
* replacements for official authority decisions
* replacements for professional legal or compliance review

Related cases may appear for suitable compliance and case-library questions.

Related cases should normally be hidden for urgent practical incident-response questions so that the answer focuses on containment, evidence preservation, escalation, recovery, and reporting assessment.

## Source and Audit Limitations

CyberLex Sweden may include local source and case audit scripts.

The source audit checks local source-file structure and metadata.

The case audit checks local case-file structure.

These audits may help identify missing sections, source links, metadata, or review notes.

They do not:

* browse the web
* confirm live legal currentness
* confirm that authority guidance is still current
* verify every case outcome
* replace legal review
* replace compliance review
* replace cybersecurity review

Audit reports are project-maintenance tools, not legal guarantees.

* first triage steps
* containment notes

* recovery notes

They are not:

* official incident records
