# CyberLex Sweden Legal Disclaimer

## Educational Prototype

CyberLex Sweden is an educational prototype created as a final school project.

It is designed to demonstrate how a source-based AI-style assistant could help users search and understand selected Swedish and EU cybersecurity law topics.

---

## Not Legal Advice

CyberLex Sweden does not provide legal advice.

The application is not a lawyer, law firm, court, public authority, or official legal service.

Answers generated by CyberLex Sweden are simplified educational explanations based on local knowledge base files.

---

## Verify with Official Sources

Users should verify important legal questions with official sources, public authorities, or qualified legal professionals.

CyberLex Sweden may include:

- simplified summaries
- incomplete information
- outdated information
- technical errors
- incorrect source matching
- limited legal coverage

---

## Limited Scope

CyberLex Sweden only covers selected topics related to cybersecurity law and digital compliance.

The current prototype includes topics such as:

- GDPR
- IMY
- personal data breaches
- NIS2
- Swedish Cybersecurity Act
- dataintrång
- EU attacks against information systems
- Cyber Resilience Act
- DORA

The application should refuse questions outside its current scope.

---

## Cybersecurity Use

CyberLex Sweden should be used for lawful cybersecurity education, awareness, and compliance learning.

It should not be used to plan, support, or justify unauthorized access, cybercrime, or harmful activity.

---

## No Guarantee

CyberLex Sweden is provided as an educational project without guarantees.

The author does not guarantee that the information is complete, current, accurate, or suitable for any specific legal or operational decision.

---

## Final Note

CyberLex Sweden is a learning project and should be treated as a prototype, not a production legal tool.
