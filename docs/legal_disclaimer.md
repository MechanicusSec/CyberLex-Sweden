# CyberLex Sweden Legal Disclaimer

## Educational Prototype

CyberLex Sweden is an educational prototype created as a final school project.

It is designed to demonstrate how a local, source-grounded assistant can help users explore selected Swedish and EU cybersecurity-law, data protection, digital compliance, cybercrime, and defensive incident-response topics.

CyberLex Sweden is not a production legal tool.

---

## Not Legal Advice

CyberLex Sweden does not provide legal advice.

The application is not:

* a lawyer
* a law firm
* a court
* a public authority
* an official legal service
* a data protection officer
* a compliance officer
* a professional incident-response provider

Answers generated by CyberLex Sweden are simplified educational explanations based on local project knowledge files.

Users should not rely on CyberLex Sweden as the only source for legal, compliance, regulatory, or cybersecurity decisions.

---

## Verify with Official Sources

Users should verify important legal, compliance, regulatory, or incident-response questions with:

* official legal sources
* relevant public authorities
* qualified legal professionals
* data protection officers
* compliance specialists
* professional cybersecurity or incident-response teams

CyberLex Sweden may include:

* simplified summaries
* incomplete information
* outdated information
* incorrect source matching
* technical errors
* limited legal coverage
* missing legal context
* prototype behavior that changes during development

---

## Limited Scope

CyberLex Sweden only covers selected topics related to cybersecurity law, data protection, digital compliance, cybercrime, and defensive cyber incident response.

The current prototype includes selected material related to:

* GDPR
* IMY
* personal data breaches
* GDPR security measures
* NIS2
* Swedish cybersecurity law
* NIS2 sector scope and applicability
* NIS2 incident reporting
* Swedish cybercrime and dataintrång
* EU attacks against information systems
* Cyber Resilience Act
* DORA
* defensive cyber incident-response support

CyberLex Sweden does not cover all Swedish law, all EU law, or all cybersecurity compliance requirements.

The application should refuse questions outside its current scope.

---

## Source and Audit Limitation

CyberLex Sweden uses local Markdown knowledge files as its source base.

The app may show source metadata, source freshness labels, source quality labels, official source links, and source context.

These features improve transparency, but they do not guarantee that the legal information is complete, current, or legally correct.

The source audit checks local file structure and metadata.

The source audit does not browse the web and does not confirm live legal currency.

---

## Cybersecurity Use

CyberLex Sweden should be used for lawful cybersecurity education, awareness, documentation practice, and compliance learning.

It should not be used to plan, support, justify, or hide:

* unauthorized access
* cybercrime
* credential theft
* exploitation of systems
* bypassing security controls
* hiding logs or traces
* malware activity
* harmful or deceptive activity

CyberLex Sweden may provide defensive guidance for supported incident-response scenarios, but this guidance is educational and does not replace professional incident handling.

---

## Incident-Response Limitation

CyberLex Sweden may generate defensive incident-response guidance and SOC-style Markdown incident summaries.

These outputs are documentation aids for learning and testing.

They are not:

* official incident reports
* legal assessments
* regulatory notifications
* forensic reports
* breach notifications to IMY
* professional incident-response instructions
* replacements for internal policies or expert review

For real incidents, users should follow their organization's internal procedures and consult qualified professionals.
