# CyberLex Sweden Legal Disclaimer

## Educational Prototype

CyberLex Sweden is an educational prototype created as a final school project.

It is designed to demonstrate how a local, source-grounded assistant can help users explore selected Swedish and EU cybersecurity-law, data protection, digital compliance, cybercrime, real-world case examples, and defensive incident-response topics.

CyberLex Sweden is not a production legal tool.

It is a learning project, not an official legal, compliance, regulatory, or incident-response service.

---

## Not Legal Advice

CyberLex Sweden does not provide legal advice.

The application is not:

* a lawyer
* a law firm
* a court
* a public authority
* an official legal service
* a data protection officer
* a compliance officer
* a professional incident-response provider
* a forensic investigation team
* a replacement for internal legal, compliance, or security procedures

Answers generated by CyberLex Sweden are simplified educational explanations based on local project knowledge files.

Users should not rely on CyberLex Sweden as the only source for legal, compliance, regulatory, or cybersecurity decisions.

For real decisions, users should verify the information with official sources and qualified professionals.

---

## Verify with Official Sources

Users should verify important legal, compliance, regulatory, or incident-response questions with:

* official legal sources
* relevant public authorities
* qualified legal professionals
* data protection officers
* compliance specialists
* professional cybersecurity or incident-response teams
* internal organizational policies and procedures

CyberLex Sweden may include:

* simplified summaries
* incomplete information
* outdated information
* incorrect source matching
* technical errors
* limited legal coverage
* missing legal context
* prototype behavior that changes during development

CyberLex Sweden may show official source links, but users are responsible for checking those sources directly when accuracy matters.

---

## Limited Scope

CyberLex Sweden only covers selected topics related to cybersecurity law, data protection, digital compliance, cybercrime, real-world case examples, and defensive cyber incident response.

The current prototype includes selected material related to:

* GDPR
* IMY
* personal data breaches
* GDPR security measures
* NIS2
* Swedish cybersecurity law
* NIS2 sector scope and applicability
* NIS2 incident reporting
* Swedish cybercrime and dataintrång
* EU attacks against information systems
* Cyber Resilience Act
* DORA
* defensive cyber incident-response support
* selected GDPR and cybersecurity-related case examples

CyberLex Sweden does not cover all Swedish law, all EU law, all cybersecurity compliance requirements, or all possible incident-response situations.

The application should refuse questions outside its current scope.

---

## Source and Audit Limitation

CyberLex Sweden uses local Markdown knowledge files as its source base.

The main source files are stored in:

```text
data/
```

The local case-library files are stored in:

```text
cases/
```

The app may show:

* source metadata
* source freshness labels
* source quality labels
* official source links
* source context
* related case examples
* case learning notes
* case source links

These features improve transparency, but they do not guarantee that the legal information is complete, current, or legally correct.

The source audit checks local source-file structure and metadata.

The case audit checks local case-file structure.

These audits do not browse the web and do not confirm live legal currency.

They also do not confirm that every law, authority decision, case outcome, guidance page, or regulatory interpretation is still current.

CyberLex Sweden includes a local case library for educational examples.

Case examples may include:

* public authority decisions

## Cybersecurity Use

CyberLex Sweden should be used for lawful cybersecurity education, awareness, documentation practice, and compliance learning.

It should not be used to plan, support, justify, or hide:

* unauthorized access
* cybercrime
* credential theft
* exploitation of systems
* bypassing security controls
* hiding logs or traces
* malware activity
* phishing
* evasion of detection
* maintaining unauthorized access
* harmful or deceptive activity

CyberLex Sweden may provide defensive guidance for supported incident-response scenarios, but this guidance is educational and does not replace professional incident handling.

The application should refuse unsafe cyber requests.

---

## Incident-Response Limitation

CyberLex Sweden may generate defensive incident-response guidance and SOC-style Markdown incident summaries.

These outputs are documentation aids for learning, testing, and structured thinking.

They are not:

* official incident reports
* legal assessments
* regulatory notifications
* forensic reports
* breach notifications to IMY
* NIS2 or Swedish cybersecurity-law incident reports
* professional incident-response instructions
* replacements for internal policies or expert review

For real incidents, users should follow their organization’s internal procedures and consult qualified professionals.

For serious cyber incidents, users should escalate through appropriate internal, legal, compliance, and cybersecurity channels.

---

## SOC Report Limitation

CyberLex Sweden may generate SOC-style Markdown reports for practical incident-response questions.

These reports may help organize:

* reported event details
* first triage steps
* containment notes
* evidence and log notes
* escalation reminders
* recovery notes
* GDPR or NIS2 assessment reminders

SOC-style reports generated by CyberLex Sweden are educational templates.

They are not official SOC reports unless reviewed, completed, and approved by the relevant organization or responsible professionals.

They should not be submitted to authorities without proper review.

---

## Language and Translation Limitation

CyberLex Sweden may support English, Swedish, and Auto language mode.

Auto language mode is based on local detection logic and may classify language incorrectly.

English and Swedish answers may not be exact translations of each other.

If legal precision matters, users should check the official source language and consult a qualified professional.

---

## Future AI or RAG Limitation

CyberLex Sweden may later add stronger retrieval, vector search, embeddings, or language-model-based answer generation.

Any future AI or RAG feature should remain source-grounded.

The system should not generate legal claims from general model memory without trusted source support.

The guiding rule is:

```text
No trusted source, no answer.
```

Future AI features may still make mistakes, retrieve the wrong source, summarize incorrectly, or miss important legal context.
